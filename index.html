Near-Infrared Hand-Vein Enhancement. University of Cape Town, BSc (Eng) Mechatronics final year project.

View project on GitHub

Project Overview

As part of the requirements of my degree it was required that I take on a self-driven research project. The aim of the project was to establish if I could undertake research and development in a practised and proper fashion and be weary of design methodologies. The project also showed if I could take an ill-defined proeject statement and produce a final engineering solution.

The project I chose was: the design and development of a near-infared imaging system which could take snapshots of the back of a human hand and determine the vein structure. This had the ultimate intention of use in a biometrtric system.

Project Background and Premis

The project was based on the phenomenon that blood with a higher concentration of CO2 will absorb infrared more readily. By illuminating tha back of a hand with an infrared light and taking a picture with an infrared sensitive camera the dark vein reigons could be isolated and then inhanced using software.

Due to the penetrative strength of near-infrared the deep veins could be detected. Near infrared must be distinguished from far infrared which would provide a heat signature rather than an absorption difference.

Results

The project turned out to be a success and was able to provide images that vasty increased the visibilty of the vein structures of the hand.
